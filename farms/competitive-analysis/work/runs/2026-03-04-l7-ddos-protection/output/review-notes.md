# Skeptic Review: L7 DDoS Protection Competitive Analysis

**Draft reviewed:** combined-draft.md
**Review date:** March 4, 2026

## Summary Verdict

The draft is well-structured and comprehensive for a two-competitor analysis, with strong sourcing for market data and Cloudflare capabilities. However, it is **not publishable as-is** due to unsourced Azure compliance claims presented as fact, an arithmetic error in the strategic recommendations, internal roadmap data bleeding into non-internal-labeled sections, and battle card objection handlers that rely on vaporware rather than current capabilities — a pattern that would collapse in a live sales call.

| 1 | Market Landscape (entire section) | **Evidence strength / Bias** | Every market statistic in this section comes from Cloudflare's own DDoS Threat Reports. There is zero independent market data (Gartner, Forrester, IDC). The draft acknowledges paywalled analyst reports in the Gaps section, but the Market Landscape reads as objective market overview when it is effectively Cloudflare's self-reported data. ## What's Good (keep as-is)

- **Comparison Matrix completeness:** All 17 dimensions are filled with no empty cells or `[NEEDS DATA]` placeholders. The matrix is detailed, well-structured, and directly actionable.
- **Source discipline for Cloudflare data:** Cloudflare capabilities are meticulously sourced with specific URLs for nearly every claim. Feature descriptions match the source files accurately.
- **Azure WAF pricing accuracy:** Pricing figures ($0.443/gateway-hour, $0.0144/CU-hour, ~$2,944/mo DDoS Protection) match the collected sources exactly.
- **Gaps & Open Questions section:** Honest and comprehensive. The draft proactively identifies its own weaknesses (no win/loss data, no Gartner data, PM resource mismatch, missing timeline). This is rare and valuable.
- **Balanced competitor depth:** Azure WAF and Cloudflare receive roughly equal depth in their deep-dive sections (comparable number of strengths, weaknesses, features, and sources).
- **Internal Context labeling:** The dedicated Internal Context section properly carries the "⚠️ Internal" warning label and correctly attributes all claims to Work IQ.

## Recommendation

The Reviser should proceed with fixes. The critical issues are structural and factual (wrong number, unsourced claims, internal bleed, weak battle cards) rather than requiring new data collection. Specifically: (1) verify and source Azure compliance certifications or remove unsourced claims, (2) fix the 8→9 count, (3) add internal-only labels to all sections containing Work IQ roadmap data, (4) reconcile the ML-detection contradiction, (5) strengthen battle card objection handlers to lead with current capabilities (MazeBolt RADAR, FedRAMP High, unified security stack) rather than future promises, and (6) either verify the Q4 2025 Cloudflare report data or flag those data points as unverified. Collection does not need to be re-run, but a targeted follow-up to fetch the Q4 2025 report and Azure compliance docs would strengthen the final output.
